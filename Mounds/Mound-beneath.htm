The Mound Beneath Me

March 2020

I found an enormous mound underneath myself.

It stays very still, and I can’t see the bottom, but I think I saw Cerberus crawl out of it and collapse with his mouths full of mud. I watched as soothsayers wondered to and fro, at neck-bone reversed: False prophets of fortune, chanting of Futures and Returns on earth. And me, above them and among them, dragging.

It tugs at my veins and for the sake of my forearms I have to look away. But it’s fusing with my bones, with my nails, with my molars. My centre of gravity pulls right towards its mass: straight ties to my navel.

It’s wallpapered on the inside and paved over on the outside. Although I haven’t investigated thoroughly, it seems to be made up of fractured Arcadia and everything that hides in plain sight - all bound together by polyester threading and petroleum adhesive.

Maybe I can reshape it;
or power wash it;
or innovate it;
maybe I can crowdsource its residents.

Maybe oceans will rise and wash it away.

Or maybe mushrooms will grow.
